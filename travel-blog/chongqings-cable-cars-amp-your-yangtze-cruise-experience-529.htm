Chongqing’s Cable Cars & Your Yangtze Cruise Experience

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Few cities blend modernity and tradition as seamlessly as Chongqing. Nestled in southwestern China, this sprawling metropolis is famous for its spicy hotpot, misty mountains, and—most notably—its iconic cable cars. Pair that with a Yangtze River cruise, and you’ve got the ultimate Chongqing adventure. Here’s why these two experiences should be at the top of your travel bucket list.

Why Chongqing’s Cable Cars Are a Must-See

A Glimpse Into the Past

Chongqing’s cable cars, or suodao (索道), aren’t just a mode of transportation—they’re a cultural relic. First introduced in the 1980s, these aerial trams were built to navigate the city’s steep hills and winding rivers. While many have been replaced by bridges and tunnels, a few still operate, offering visitors a nostalgic ride with breathtaking views.

The Best Views of the City

Hop on the Yangtze River Cable Car (长江索道), and you’ll glide over the bustling streets and waterways below. The ride connects Chongqing’s Yuzhong District to Nan’an District, treating passengers to panoramic vistas of the city’s skyline, the confluence of the Yangtze and Jialing Rivers, and the surrounding mountains. For photographers, sunset rides are pure magic—golden light reflecting off the water as the city begins to glow with neon lights.

A Thrilling Urban Adventure

If you love heights, this is for you. The cable cars move at a steady pace, but the open windows (yes, they’re not fully enclosed!) let in the breeze, making the experience even more exhilarating. Locals use them daily, so you’ll get an authentic slice of Chongqing life.

Enhancing Your Trip with a Yangtze River Cruise

The Perfect Complement to Chongqing’s Cable Cars

After soaking in the city’s aerial views, take your adventure to the water. A Yangtze River cruise is the ideal way to explore the region’s natural beauty. Most cruises depart from Chongqing, winding through the Three Gorges—Qutang, Wu, and Xiling—before reaching Yichang or even further downstream.

What to Expect on Board

Modern Yangtze cruises offer luxury amenities, from spacious cabins with private balconies to gourmet dining featuring Sichuan cuisine. Evening entertainment often includes traditional performances, tai chi sessions at sunrise, and lectures on local history.

Must-See Stops Along the Way

  • Fengdu Ghost City – A fascinating (and slightly spooky) ancient temple complex dedicated to the afterlife.
  • Shibaozhai Pagoda – A 12-story wooden pagoda clinging to a riverside cliff.
  • Three Gorges Dam – One of the world’s largest hydroelectric projects, offering an impressive engineering spectacle.

Combining Both Experiences for the Ultimate Chongqing Journey

How to Plan Your Itinerary

  1. Start with the Cable Cars – Ride them early to avoid crowds and capture the best photos.
  2. Explore Chongqing’s Highlights – Visit Hongya Cave, Ciqikou Ancient Town, and indulge in a fiery hotpot feast.
  3. Board Your Cruise – Most ships depart in the evening, so time it right to see Chongqing’s skyline fade into the distance.

Pro Tips for Travelers

  • Book in Advance – Both cable cars and cruises can get crowded, especially during peak seasons.
  • Pack Layers – Chongqing’s weather can be unpredictable, and the river breeze can get chilly.
  • Try Local Snacks – Grab some xiaomian (spicy noodles) or laziji (fried chicken with chilies) before boarding.

Chongqing’s cable cars and a Yangtze cruise aren’t just activities—they’re unforgettable experiences that showcase the city’s dynamic spirit and China’s awe-inspiring landscapes. Whether you’re a history buff, an adrenaline seeker, or a food lover, this duo promises an adventure like no other.
