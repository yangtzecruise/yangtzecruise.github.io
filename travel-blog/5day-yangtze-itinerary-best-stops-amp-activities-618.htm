5-Day Yangtze Itinerary: Best Stops & Activities

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

The Yangtze River, China’s longest waterway, offers an unforgettable journey through breathtaking landscapes, ancient towns, and modern marvels. Whether you’re cruising on a luxury ship or exploring riverside gems, this 5-day itinerary covers the best stops and activities along the Yangtze.

Day 1: Chongqing – Gateway to the Yangtze

Explore Ciqikou Ancient Town

Start your adventure in Chongqing, a sprawling metropolis where modernity meets tradition. Wander through Ciqikou Ancient Town, a well-preserved Ming and Qing dynasty village. Narrow alleys, teahouses, and local snacks like mala tang (spicy hot pot) make it a must-visit.

Visit Hongya Cave

Next, head to Hongya Cave, a stunning stilted building complex overlooking the river. By night, its illuminated facade creates a magical atmosphere. Don’t miss the chance to try Chongqing’s famous hotpot here!

Board Your Cruise

Most Yangtze cruises depart from Chongqing in the evening. Settle into your cabin and enjoy a welcome dinner as the ship sets sail.

Day 2: Fengdu Ghost City & Shibaozhai

Fengdu Ghost City

Wake up to misty river views before docking at Fengdu Ghost City, a Taoist-themed attraction filled with eerie statues and temples. Legend says it’s where spirits are judged in the afterlife—spooky but fascinating!

Shibaozhai Pagoda

In the afternoon, visit Shibaozhai Pagoda, a 12-story wooden tower built against a cliff. Climb to the top for panoramic river views or explore the nearby temple.

Onboard Activities

Back on the cruise, enjoy cultural performances, a captain’s welcome party, or relax on the deck under the stars.

Day 3: The Three Gorges & Shennong Stream

Qutang Gorge

The morning brings the first of the Three Gorges—Qutang Gorge, the shortest but most dramatic with steep cliffs towering over the river.

Wu Gorge & Shennong Stream

Next, glide through Wu Gorge, known for its serene beauty and misty peaks. Disembark for a Shennong Stream excursion, where traditional sampan boats take you through emerald waters flanked by lush forests.

Xiling Gorge

By afternoon, enter Xiling Gorge, the longest of the three. Watch for whirlpools and rapid currents as the ship navigates this historic passage.

Evening Relaxation

Unwind with tai chi on deck or a spa treatment as the ship continues downstream.

Day 4: Three Gorges Dam & Yichang

Three Gorges Dam

Today’s highlight is the Three Gorges Dam, the world’s largest hydroelectric project. Tour the observation deck and learn about its engineering feats and environmental impact.

Tribe of the Three Gorges

After the dam, visit the Tribe of the Three Gorges, a cultural park showcasing local traditions, dance performances, and handicrafts.

Disembark in Yichang

Your cruise ends in Yichang, a charming city with a slower pace. Stroll along the riverfront or visit the Yichang Museum to delve deeper into the region’s history.

Day 5: Optional Extension to Wuhan or Shanghai

Wuhan Highlights

If time allows, take a high-speed train to Wuhan. Visit the Yellow Crane Tower for poetic river views or explore East Lake, China’s largest urban lake.

Shanghai Detour

Alternatively, fly to Shanghai and cap off your trip with a Huangpu River cruise, offering skyline views of the Bund and Pudong.

Pro Tips for Your Yangtze Adventure

  • Best Time to Go: Spring (April-May) and autumn (September-October) offer mild weather and fewer crowds.
  • Packing Essentials: Layers for changing temperatures, comfortable shoes, and a good camera.
  • Booking Tips: Reserve cruises early, especially during peak seasons. Opt for a balcony cabin for the best views.

This 5-day Yangtze itinerary blends natural wonders, cultural gems, and modern engineering—a journey you’ll cherish forever.
