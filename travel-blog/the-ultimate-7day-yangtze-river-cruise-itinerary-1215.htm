The Ultimate 7-Day Yangtze River Cruise Itinerary

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

Few travel experiences rival the majesty of a Yangtze River cruise. As China’s longest river and the third-longest in the world, the Yangtze offers a breathtaking blend of natural wonders, ancient culture, and modern engineering feats. This 7-day itinerary is designed to showcase the very best of the river, from the towering Three Gorges to the futuristic Three Gorges Dam. Whether you're a history buff, a nature lover, or simply seeking relaxation with stunning views, this journey promises unforgettable moments.

Day 1: Embark in Chongqing – The Mountain City

Your adventure begins in Chongqing, a sprawling metropolis known for its spicy hotpot and misty hills. Before boarding your cruise ship, take time to explore:

Hongya Cave

This iconic stilted building complex is a maze of shops, teahouses, and restaurants. At night, the illuminated structure reflects beautifully on the river.

Ciqikou Ancient Town

A well-preserved Ming and Qing dynasty village, Ciqikou is perfect for sampling local snacks like mala tang (spicy skewers) and browsing handicrafts.

In the evening, board your luxury vessel and settle in as the ship sets sail. Enjoy a welcome dinner featuring Sichuan cuisine while the city’s skyline fades into the distance.

Day 2: Fengdu – The Ghost City

Wake up to serene river views as your ship approaches Fengdu, known as the "Ghost City" of Chinese folklore.

Fengdu Ghost City

This eerie temple complex is filled with statues and artwork depicting the afterlife in Taoist tradition. Climb the steps to the top for panoramic views of the Yangtze.

Shore Excursion Options

  • Optional: Visit the newly developed Fengdu New Town, a striking contrast to the ancient sites.

Back on board, attend a cultural lecture or relax on the sun deck as the ship continues toward the Three Gorges.

Day 3: The Three Gorges – Nature’s Masterpiece

Today is the highlight of your cruise: sailing through the legendary Three Gorges—Qutang, Wu, and Xiling.

Qutang Gorge

The shortest but most dramatic gorge, where cliffs rise sharply from the water. Don’t miss the Kui Gate, a narrow passage immortalized in traditional paintings.

Wu Gorge

Known for its misty peaks and lush greenery, Wu Gorge is often called the "Gallery of the Yangtze." Keep an eye out for the Goddess Peak, a rock formation resembling a kneeling maiden.

Shennong Stream (Small Gorges)

Disembark for a small boat excursion up the Shennong Stream. Local Tujia guides row traditional peapod boats while sharing stories of the region’s ethnic heritage.

Day 4: Three Gorges Dam & Xiling Gorge

Three Gorges Dam

The world’s largest hydroelectric dam is a marvel of modern engineering. Tour the visitor center to learn about its impact on the region and enjoy sweeping views from the observation deck.

Xiling Gorge

The longest of the three gorges, Xiling offers rugged landscapes and thrilling rapids (now tamed by the dam).

Evening Activity

Attend a crew performance featuring traditional music and dance, or sip a cocktail under the stars.

Day 5: Yichang & The Lesser Three Gorges

Yichang Disembarkation (Optional)

Some cruises end here, but if you’re continuing, explore Yichang’s China Three Gorges Museum before reboarding.

Lesser Three Gorges

A tributary of the Yangtze, this area is quieter and even more pristine. Drift past waterfalls, hanging coffins, and rare wildlife like golden monkeys.

Day 6: Shibaozhai – The Pearl of the Yangtze

Shibaozhai Pagoda

This 12-story wooden pagoda leans dramatically against a cliff. Climb to the top for breathtaking river views and visit the Qing Dynasty temple at its base.

Local Market Visit

Stroll through a riverside market where farmers sell fresh produce and handmade crafts.

Day 7: Disembark in Shanghai (or Wuhan)

Depending on your cruise line, your journey concludes in either Shanghai or Wuhan.

Shanghai Extension

If ending in Shanghai, spend extra days exploring:
- The Bund: Colonial-era architecture meets futuristic skyscrapers.
- Yu Garden: A tranquil Ming Dynasty garden in the heart of the city.

Wuhan Highlights

For those disembarking in Wuhan:
- Yellow Crane Tower: A poetic landmark with 1,800 years of history.
- East Lake: China’s largest urban lake, perfect for cycling or boating.

Pro Tips for Your Yangtze Cruise

Best Time to Go

  • Spring (April-May): Mild weather and blooming scenery.
  • Fall (September-October): Clear skies and comfortable temperatures.

Packing Essentials

  • Layers (mornings can be chilly, afternoons warm).
  • Comfortable walking shoes for shore excursions.
  • Binoculars for wildlife spotting.

Choosing Your Cruise Line

Luxury options like Victoria Cruises and Century Paragon offer top-tier amenities, while budget-friendly lines like President Cruises provide great value.

The Yangtze River is more than a waterway—it’s a living tapestry of China’s past and present. From the haunting beauty of Fengdu to the awe-inspiring Three Gorges Dam, every moment of this 7-day journey tells a story. Whether you’re gliding through emerald gorges or toasting to the sunset on deck, this cruise is the ultimate way to experience the heart of China.
