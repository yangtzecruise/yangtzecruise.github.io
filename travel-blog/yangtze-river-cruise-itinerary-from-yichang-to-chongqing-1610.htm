Yangtze River Cruise Itinerary: From Yichang to Chongqing

Home / Travel Blog / Blog Details

The Yangtze River, China’s longest and the world’s third-longest river, offers one of the most breathtaking cruise experiences in Asia. A journey from Yichang to Chongqing is a voyage through time, culture, and nature, blending modern comforts with ancient wonders. Whether you're a history buff, a nature lover, or simply seeking relaxation, this itinerary promises unforgettable moments.

Why Choose the Yichang to Chongqing Route?

This stretch of the Yangtze is famous for the Three Gorges—Qutang, Wu, and Xiling—each with its own dramatic landscapes and legends. The cruise also passes through the monumental Three Gorges Dam, a feat of modern engineering. Unlike shorter routes, the Yichang-Chongqing journey allows deeper immersion into the region’s beauty and culture.

Highlights of the Itinerary

  1. Three Gorges Dam – A must-see engineering marvel.
  2. Shennong Stream – A serene tributary with emerald waters.
  3. Fengdu Ghost City – A mystical site steeped in folklore.
  4. Chongqing’s Vibrant Nightlife – The perfect finale to your cruise.

Day-by-Day Breakdown

Day 1: Embarkation in Yichang

Your adventure begins in Yichang, a gateway to the Yangtze. Most cruises depart in the evening, giving you time to explore the city. Don’t miss:

  • Yichang Museum – Learn about the region’s history.
  • The Bund – A scenic riverside promenade.

After boarding, enjoy a welcome dinner as the ship sets sail.

Day 2: Three Gorges Dam & Xiling Gorge

The morning brings you to the Three Gorges Dam, the world’s largest hydroelectric power station. Witness the ship passing through the massive locks—an impressive experience.

In the afternoon, enter Xiling Gorge, the longest of the Three Gorges. Towering cliffs and misty peaks create a surreal backdrop.

Optional Excursion: Tribe of the Three Gorges

A cultural performance showcasing local traditions and music.

Day 3: Wu Gorge & Shennong Stream

Wu Gorge is renowned for its quiet beauty and steep, forested mountains. The narrow river passage feels almost mystical, especially when shrouded in morning fog.

Later, transfer to a smaller boat for the Shennong Stream excursion. Drift past hanging coffins (an ancient burial practice) and spot wildlife along the untouched shores.

Day 4: Qutang Gorge & Fengdu Ghost City

Qutang Gorge, the shortest but most dramatic of the Three Gorges, features sheer cliffs that seem to rise straight from the water.

In the afternoon, visit Fengdu Ghost City, a temple complex dedicated to the afterlife in Chinese mythology. Walk through eerie statues and learn about underworld legends.

Day 5: Arrival in Chongqing

Disembark in Chongqing, a sprawling metropolis known for its spicy hotpot and futuristic skyline. If time allows:

  • Ciqi Kou Ancient Town – A well-preserved historical district.
  • Hongya Cave – A stilted building complex glowing at night.

Travel Tips for the Best Experience

Best Time to Cruise

  • Spring (April-May) – Pleasant weather, blooming scenery.
  • Autumn (September-October) – Clear skies, ideal for photography.

Choosing Your Cruise Ship

Luxury options like Victoria Cruises or Century Paragon offer top-tier amenities, while budget-friendly ships still provide comfort.

Packing Essentials

  • Comfortable walking shoes for shore excursions.
  • Layers—mornings on deck can be chilly.
  • Binoculars for wildlife spotting.

Beyond the Cruise: Extending Your Trip

Consider adding these destinations:

  • Chengdu – Visit the Panda Research Base.
  • Zhangjiajie – The inspiration for Avatar’s floating mountains.

A Yangtze River cruise from Yichang to Chongqing is more than a trip—it’s a journey through China’s soul. From the raw power of the Three Gorges Dam to the tranquility of Shennong Stream, every moment is a postcard waiting to be captured.
